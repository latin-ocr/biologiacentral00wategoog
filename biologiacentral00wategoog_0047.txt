The insects belonging to the genus Arescue are above the medium size, brilliantly
coloured, and are so variable in tint and markings that in a long series of individuale
belonging to the same species, it is comparatively rare to find two specimens of
precisely the same colour aud pattern. From their abundance and variability it is fair
to assume that the conditione under which these insects live are greatly in excess of the
absolute requirements of the species, and that the additional vital energy thus produed
expends itself in the development of innumerable variatione from the original type of

coloration.
